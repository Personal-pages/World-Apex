Govt to Hold All-Party Meet on Pahalgam Attack on April 24

Published: April 24, 2025 | 10:15 PM IST

gavel National Unity in Response to Terror

Following the tragic attack in the scenic town of Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir, the central government has announced an all-party meeting on April 24 to discuss the nation's collective response. The decision was made amid increasing calls for a coordinated strategy to address escalating threats in the region.

security Strengthening Security Architecture

Home Minister Amit Shah confirmed that security reviews and intelligence updates will be shared during the meet. The agenda will include measures to tighten border surveillance, modernize counter-terrorism operations, and evaluate the current deployment of forces in vulnerable zones near Pahalgam.

groups All-Party Participation

Invitations have been extended to leaders from the Indian National Congress, Aam Aadmi Party, Trinamool Congress, and regional players like the PDP and National Conference. The government seeks bipartisan cooperation to avoid politicization and ensure strategic alignment across states and parties.

emoji_flags Opposition's Demands

Opposition parties have demanded accountability for the intelligence lapse and stressed the importance of rebuilding public trust. Congress leader Mallikarjun Kharge stated, “This must not be another closed-door conversation. The public deserves clarity and concrete action.”

location_on Pahalgam’s Strategic Importance

Pahalgam, while known for its beauty and as a key point on the Amarnath Yatra route, has increasingly been on the radar for extremist activity. Analysts suggest the attackers aimed to destabilize the tourism economy and provoke unrest ahead of the peak pilgrimage season.

insights Experts Weigh In

Security experts believe that the rise in attacks is connected to a shift in tactics by cross-border terror outfits, now focusing on soft targets in high-visibility zones. “The symbolism of attacking Pahalgam isn’t lost — it’s psychological warfare,” said defense analyst Lt. Gen. (Retd.) R.S. Panwar.

emoji_people Citizens Demand Reassurance

Locals and tourists have raised concerns about safety and uncertainty in the region. The meeting is expected to propose confidence-building measures including increased checkpoints, local helplines, drone surveillance, and emergency response training in hotels and yatra camps.

schedule Expected Outcomes

A white paper outlining the central and state governments' joint counter-terror roadmap is expected after the meet. There is speculation that the National Security Council may approve a region-specific “Red Zone Security Doctrine” to classify and act against high-risk areas with real-time coordination.

public Global Watch

With international tourism and investment eyeing Jammu and Kashmir, the attack has drawn global concern. Embassies of the UK, US, and Germany have issued travel advisories, urging the government to ensure security and fast investigation. The meeting outcome may influence foreign policy responses as well.

trending_up Looking Ahead

As India prepares for a potentially volatile summer in the region, the April 24 meeting could mark a turning point in redefining anti-terror coordination between political stakeholders. All eyes will be on New Delhi as the nation waits for concrete results beyond promises and politics.
