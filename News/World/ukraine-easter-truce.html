Zelenskyy Says Ukraine Will Observe Easter Truce, Alleges Violations by Russia

Published: April 10, 2025 | 11:02 PM

peace Ceasefire Commitment

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy affirmed Ukraine's readiness to observe the Easter truce proposed by Russia, viewing it as a symbolic opportunity to pause hostilities during the sacred holiday.

report_problem Truce Violations Accused

Despite the announcement, Zelenskyy accused Moscow of violating the ceasefire terms within hours of its declaration. Ukrainian officials claimed shelling resumed on several frontline regions, undermining the spirit of the truce.

visibility Call for Global Watch

Zelenskyy called on international observers and humanitarian organizations to closely monitor ceasefire implementation. He stressed that such monitoring is vital to maintaining transparency and accountability.

public Global Implications

The proposed truce, if upheld, could open a narrow window for renewed diplomatic engagement. However, continued violations may harden stances on both sides and further escalate tensions in the region.
