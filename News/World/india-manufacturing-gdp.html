India Targets 23% GDP Share from Manufacturing, Boosted by Sunrise Sectors

Published: April 21, 2025 | 3:45 PM

factory India’s Big Manufacturing Push

In a bold move to reshape its economic trajectory, India has announced an ambitious plan to raise the manufacturing sector's share of its Gross Domestic Product (GDP) to 23%, nearly double the current contribution. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman emphasized this vision during the annual Economic Growth Conclave, citing the urgent need to transform India into a global hub for high-value, sustainable manufacturing.

insights Sunrise Sectors Lead the Charge

Central to this transformation are "sunrise sectors" — rapidly growing industries with long-term national and global relevance. These include semiconductors, green hydrogen, electric mobility, advanced electronics, and clean energy. The government has already launched Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) schemes in several of these sectors, aiming to attract both domestic entrepreneurs and global giants.

public Global Competitiveness and Self-Reliance

Sitharaman highlighted that building competitive manufacturing strength is key to making India self-reliant while also integrating it deeper into global supply chains. “We are not only creating jobs; we are setting the foundation for global leadership in next-gen technologies,” she said. The goal is to position India as a viable alternative to existing manufacturing powerhouses like China, especially in the electronics and semiconductor space.

electric_bolt Green Energy and Sustainable Focus

With climate change a growing concern, the government’s push includes green manufacturing practices and carbon neutrality initiatives. The focus on green hydrogen and battery production aligns with India's climate commitments under the Paris Agreement. Industrial zones are being reimagined as eco-smart manufacturing corridors, with clean water access, waste management, and renewable power integration.

engineering Skilling the Workforce

To support this structural shift, the Ministry of Skill Development is working closely with private players to train over 5 million workers over the next 3 years. Specialized programs will focus on chip design, robotics, automation, and AI-integrated systems to prepare the labor force for future-ready jobs in sunrise industries.

currency_rupee Investments and Tax Benefits

The government plans to provide tax incentives, faster environmental clearances, and export duty waivers to attract investments. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) inflows into manufacturing have already surged in the past fiscal year, signaling global interest in India’s evolving industrial story.

apartment State-Level Action

States like Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, and Telangana are rolling out state-specific manufacturing corridors. With digital infrastructure, investor summits, and industrial townships, these states are tailoring their policies to specific industries — EVs in Gujarat, semiconductors in Telangana, and AI-based robotics in Tamil Nadu.

psychology Expert Opinions

Industry experts see this as a decisive shift. “India’s economic future depends on scaling up precision manufacturing, not just assembly,” said Anil Mathur, Chair of IndiaTech Council. “This focus on high-value sectors like semiconductors is a move in the right direction — but execution and consistency will be the key.”

eco Balancing Growth with Environment

Environmental groups have cautiously welcomed the move, urging the government to ensure that rapid industrialization does not repeat past mistakes. Organizations like TERI and CSE have recommended green certification norms and independent sustainability audits for all upcoming manufacturing zones.

timeline What’s Next?

The Finance Ministry is expected to release a detailed roadmap by July 2025, including timelines, state-wise targets, and an online dashboard to track the sector's performance in real-time. With India’s youth population surging and demand for global decoupling from China rising, this could mark a historic chapter for India’s economic evolution — if it’s implemented right.
